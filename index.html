PickleStack Privacy Policy

Last updated: June 30, 2026

PickleStack is a pickleball scorekeeping app for iPhone and Apple Watch. This page describes what data the app uses and how.

No backend, no account system

PickleStack has no server of its own. Your match data — player names, scores, and stats — lives in your own iCloud account, using Apple's CloudKit. The developer never sees or has access to this data.

Groups

If you create or join a Group to share matches with friends, that data lives in a CloudKit Shared Zone visible only to people you've explicitly invited through Apple's standard sharing flow. Nothing is shared until you create or join a group.

Apple Health

PickleStack writes a workout session (heart rate, calories) to Apple Health during a match, for your own benefit. This data is never read for advertising or analytics, never shared with anyone, and never leaves your device except to Apple Health itself.

Motion & Fitness

Used only to detect when play has paused, entirely on-device. Never transmitted anywhere.

Bluetooth

Used only to connect players' iPhones to each other during a match, directly device-to-device. No internet connection or account is required, and nothing about this connection is sent to the developer or anyone else.

Contacts

If you invite someone using your Contacts, the app uses Apple's built-in contact picker — you choose one contact at a time. PickleStack never reads or uploads your full address book.

Third-party services

PickleStack does not use any third-party analytics, advertising, or tracking SDKs. Your data is never sold or shared with third parties.

Data deletion

Because all your data lives in your own iCloud account, you can delete it at any time via your device's Settings → [your name] → iCloud, or by deleting the app. There is no separate account to delete, because there is no separate account.

Children's privacy

PickleStack is not directed at children and does not knowingly collect data from anyone under 13.

Changes to this policy

This page will be updated if our data practices change.
