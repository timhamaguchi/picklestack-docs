Setting Up a Match

Last updated: July 19, 2026

The host always sets up the match on their phone first — pick the players, tap Start. What happens on everyone's watches depends on who has one.

Just one watch

  1. Add every player's name — even players without the app.
  2. Tap Start.
  3. Your watch scores for everyone — tap for whichever team wins the rally.

No app, no Watch? No problem — your watch keeps score for the whole match, and it shows where everyone should stand: you, your partner, even the other team. Call it out before each serve.

Others can join too

  1. Host sets up & starts the match — same first step.
  2. Each other player opens PickleStack on their own watch and taps Join.
  3. Their watch finds the host nearby and connects.
  4. Everyone scores from their own wrist, perfectly in sync.

Mixing is fine too — some players join from their watch, others don't have one. Whoever's watch they're sharing just scores for them.

Choosing who serves first

Before a game's first serve, a swap button appears between each pair on the watch court — tap it to change who starts on the right (that player serves first). It disappears once the first rally is played and returns at the start of every game. Serve passes to the other team automatically each new game, per the official rules.

Hosting but not playing?

Set up the match without yourself and tap Start — your phone is the scoreboard, and your watch shows Hosting and stays out of the way. The players keep score from their watches.

Lending your watch to a player? Tap Play, and they pick their spot — shown as court initials, just like the tiles on court. Back returns the watch to Hosting.
